  • Exclude planned maintenance (with notice)

2. No compensation for downtime HIGH
Category: Service Level
Key Negotiation Points:

  • Termination right for severe failures
  • Refund option for repeated failures
  • Credits applied automatically
3. Best effort service delivery HIGH
Category: Service Level
Key Negotiation Points:
  • Replace 'best effort' with specific metrics
  • Request written SLA in contract

Alternative Contract Language

The following sections provide recommended contract language to address identified risks. Work with your procurement and legal teams to propose these modifications to the vendor.

1. Level No Sla Provided (Service)

❌ Problematic Language:
Services are provided 'as is' with no uptime or availability guarantees.
✅ Recommended Language:
Vendor guarantees [99.9%] Monthly Uptime for the production environment. 'Monthly Uptime' is calculated as: (Total minutes in month - Downtime minutes) / Total minutes in month. Planned maintenance (with 7 days' notice) is excluded from Downtime.

2. Level No Compensation For Downtime (Service)

❌ Problematic Language:
Vendor's sole liability for service failures is to use reasonable efforts to restore service.
✅ Recommended Language:
If Monthly Uptime falls below the SLA, Customer shall receive Service Credits as follows: (a) <99.9% - 10% credit, (b) <99.0% - 25% credit, (c) <95.0% - 50% credit. For outages exceeding 72 consecutive hours or 3 SLA failures in 6 months, Customer may terminate without penalty and receive full refund of prepaid fees.

3. Level Best Effort Service Delivery (Service)

❌ Problematic Language:
Vendor will make commercially reasonable efforts to maintain service availability.
✅ Recommended Language:
Vendor commits to the specific Service Levels set forth in Exhibit B, including uptime guarantees, response times, and performance metrics. Vendor shall implement redundancy, backup systems, and disaster recovery procedures to meet these commitments.

4. Terms Unilateral Price Increases (Pricing)

❌ Problematic Language:
Vendor may modify pricing at any time in its sole discretion.
✅ Recommended Language:
Pricing shall remain fixed for the Initial Term. For renewal terms, Vendor may increase pricing by no more than [5%] annually or the Consumer Price Index (CPI), whichever is lower, with at least 90 days' advance written notice. Customer may terminate without penalty if price increase exceeds this threshold.

5. Terms Auto-Renewal With New Pricing (Pricing)

❌ Problematic Language:
Subscription automatically renews at the then-current pricing.
✅ Recommended Language:
Subscription shall automatically renew at the pricing in effect at the commencement of the current term, unless Vendor provides written notice of price changes at least 90 days prior to renewal. Customer may opt-out of renewal within 30 days of price change notice without penalty.

Negotiation Strategy

  • Prioritize based on business impact: Focus negotiation efforts on clauses that pose the highest risk to your organization. Use the risk assessment scores to prioritize: SLAs and pricing are typically highest priority.
  • Request redlines in writing: Always request and review vendor responses to your proposed changes in writing. Verbal commitments are not enforceable.
  • Benchmark against industry standards: Reference industry-standard contract terms and competitor offerings during negotiations. Use the comparison data from multiple vendor assessments.
  • Bundle requests: Present multiple requested changes together rather than one-at-a-time. This gives you negotiation flexibility and shows you've done comprehensive review.
  • Know your alternatives: Have backup vendor options identified. This strengthens your negotiating position and provides genuine alternatives if negotiations fail.
  • Escalate strategically: If initial vendor contact (sales, legal) won't negotiate key terms, escalate to management. Express willingness to walk away if critical issues aren't addressed.
  • Document everything: Keep records of all negotiations, proposed changes, vendor responses, and final agreed terms. This protects you if disputes arise later.
  • Use proof of concept (POC) leverage: Negotiate contract terms before or during POC period when vendor is most motivated to close the deal.

About This Assessment

Methodology

This assessment uses a validated framework based on systematic content analysis of 61 vendor contracts from major software providers. The 100-point weighted scoring system prioritizes categories based on empirical risk data:

  • Service Level Agreements (25 points) - Highest risk category (81.1% of SLA clauses were high-risk)
  • Pricing Terms (25 points) - Second highest risk (66.1% high-risk)
  • Termination/Exit (20 points) - Moderate risk (41.3% high-risk)
  • Data Portability (15 points) - Often under-addressed
  • Support Obligations (15 points) - Lower average risk (25.0% high-risk)

Limitations

This automated assessment identifies common lock-in mechanisms but cannot replace professional legal review. Always consult with qualified legal counsel before signing contracts, especially for high-value or business-critical services.
